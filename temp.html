convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit

Celsius: Developed by Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius in 1742, this scale sets 0°C as the freezing point of water and 100°C as its boiling point. It's based on the decimal system, making calculations easier.

Fahrenheit: Developed by German physicist Daniel Fahrenheit in 1714, this scale sets 32°F as the freezing point of water and 212°F as its boiling point. The origin of these values is a bit more obscure!

Fahrenheit = (Celsius x 9/5) + 32

For example, to convert 20 degrees Celsius to Fahrenheit:

Multiply Celsius by 9/5: 20 (Celsius) * 9/5 = 36

Add 32: 36 + 32 = 68

Therefore, 20 degrees Celsius is equal to 68 degrees Fahrenheit.

convert from Celsius to Kelvin

Celsius: Developed by Anders Celsius, this scale sets 0°C as the freezing point of water and 100°C as its boiling point. It's widely used for everyday temperatures.

Kelvin: Named after William Thomson, Lord Kelvin, this thermodynamic temperature scale sets absolute zero (the point where all molecular motion stops) at 0 K. The freezing point of water on the Kelvin scale is approximately 273.15 K.

Kelvin = Celsius + 273.15

For example, to convert 20 degrees Celsius to Kelvin:

Add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature: 20°C + 273.15 = 293.15 K

Therefore, 20°C is equal to 293.15 K.
